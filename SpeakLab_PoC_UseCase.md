# SpeakLab Proof of Concept (PoC) & Use Case

### 1. One-Line Pitch
SpeakLab is an interactive, gamified language platform that connects EFL learners worldwide for live, real-time voice conversations paired with AI-powered feedback.

### 2. The Use Case
- **WHO:** B1-B2 level EFL (English as a Foreign Language) learners in non-English speaking countries who study independently or supplement classes, seeking real conversation practice.
- **PROBLEM:** They have no affordable or accessible way to get live speaking practice with targeted feedback to break through the "intermediate plateau."
- **HOW SpeakLab SOLVES IT:** The user opens the app, reviews the daily topic and vocabulary, and joins a matchmaking queue. They are connected instantly to a peer for a timed voice call, with real-time translation tools and picture-describing prompts available during the chat. Afterward, they receive badges, rate their peer, and complete an AI-generated quiz based on words they translated during the call.
- **WHY NOW:** The barrier to entry for building real-time audio and integrating low-cost, low-latency AI models (like Gemini Flash and DeepSeek) has plummeted, while remote learning habits post-COVID remain strong.

### 3. Proof of Concept — What Actually Works Today
- **Real-time matchmaking:** *Working.* Uses Firestore-based queuing (`MatchMaking.js`) to connect users.
- **Live voice calls:** *Working.* Powered by Agora RTC (`Chat.jsx`) with a 30-minute time limit for the MVP.
- **Daily topic system:** *Working.* Features 30 rotating daily topics complete with vocabulary, idioms, and tiered questions (`weeklyContent.js`).
- **AI post-call analysis:** *Partially built / Disabled.* The code for full audio analysis using Gemini 2.0 Flash (`analyzeWithGemini.js`) and OpenAI Whisper + GPT-4o-mini (`functions/index.js`) exists, but background speech capture is currently disabled in the UI for the 1-month MVP promotion. However, a post-call vocabulary quiz generated by DeepSeek API based on the user's translated words is *Working* (`aiQuizGenerator.js`).
- **Speech-to-text pipeline:** *Partially built.* Native Capacitor SpeechRecognition and Web Speech APIs are coded but commented out to save costs and avoid battery-saving OS interruptions for the MVP.
- **Picture Describing feature:** *Working.* Integrated as a component (`PictureDescribing.js`) within the call interface.
- **Translation widget + word history:** *Working.* Users can translate words mid-call and review their history (`TranslateWidget.js`, `WordHistoryPanel.js`).
- **Badge/gamification system:** *Working.* Streaks, call minutes, and peer ratings unlock specific badges and potential bonus minutes (`BadgeSystem.jsx`).
- **Telegram Mini App integration:** *Working.* Users can join and launch the web app directly from a Telegram bot (`functions/index.js` webhooks and Telegram context setup).
- **Native Android APK status:** *In progress.* Built as a PWA running through Capacitor (`capacitor.config.ts`); the initial white screen issue is resolved, but background microphone permissions and native speech-to-text are still being optimized.

### 4. Technical Architecture
**Stack:** React (Frontend), Firebase (Auth, Firestore, Cloud Functions), Agora RTC (Audio streaming), Gemini 2.0 Flash / OpenAI / DeepSeek (AI features).
The application operates as a Progressive Web App (PWA) with a dedicated Telegram Mini App integration, and is currently wrapped via Capacitor for an upcoming Native Android APK release on Google Play.

### 5. Known Limitations
- **Current user base size:** Pre-launch/Early MVP stage. Matchmaking requires concurrent users, which is challenging before reaching critical mass (the "cold start" problem).
- **Speech-to-text reliability caveats:** Disabled in the current MVP due to Android battery-saving auto-stop issues and varying cross-browser support for the Web Speech API.
- **Cost model at scale:** While Agora and Firebase have generous free tiers, turning on full real-time AI audio analysis (Whisper + Gemini) for every call will incur significant costs at scale.
- **Features not yet built:** Premium tiers, detailed level-based filtering during matchmaking, and structured "Course Modes" are future concepts, not current realities.
